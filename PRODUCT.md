# Product

## Register

product

## Users

LandlordHours is for rental-property owners and couples who need a reliable, low-friction record of real estate activity hours for tax qualification planning. They are often logging work between property visits, repairs, bookkeeping, tenant communication, and calendar events, so the app must be fast, understandable, and trustworthy on a phone.

## Product Purpose

The app helps users track rental activity hours by property, category, participant, and tax year, then shows progress toward Real Estate Professional Status, material participation tests, and the 50% rule. Success means a user can confidently log time as work happens, see what still needs attention, and export or review a coherent record without needing to decode tax rules every time they open the app.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, precise, calm. The interface should feel approachable for non-experts while still respecting the seriousness of tax documentation. It should reduce anxiety by making status, next steps, and evidence quality obvious.

## Anti-references

Avoid sterile tax software, generic enterprise dashboards, over-decorated finance apps, and novelty UI that makes ordinary logging feel unfamiliar. Do not lean on navy-blue legacy branding, dense legal copy, placeholder illustrations, or decorative gradients that compete with the user’s task.

## Design Principles

1. Make qualification status legible at a glance, with the supporting math one tap away.
2. Keep logging faster than remembering the details later.
3. Explain tax concepts in operational language, not legalese.
4. Treat spouse, property, category, and calendar context as first-class evidence fields.
5. Use a consistent soft, modern SwiftUI system that works equally well in light and dark mode.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Support Dynamic Type, VoiceOver labels for tax metrics and progress rings, reduced motion alternatives for celebrations and transitions, and color-independent status indicators for goal progress, errors, warnings, and success states.
